# Label schema: financial portrayal (document-level)

Use these three labels for **how the article portrays the company or market narrative**, not generic social “happy/sad” sentiment.

| Label | Meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| **positive** | The piece frames the company or outlook favorably (growth, strength, opportunity, beat expectations, constructive analyst view). |
| **negative** | Unfavorable framing (decline, risk, scandal, miss, downgrade, litigation stress, bearish narrative). |
| **neutral** | Factual, balanced, or mixed without a clear positive or negative lean; routine reporting. |

**Guidelines**

- Judge the **dominant tone** of the document (or the excerpt you label) toward the **main** companies or the overall market story.
- If positive and negative angles are both strong but one clearly dominates, pick that label; if they balance, use **neutral**.
- Headline-only labels are allowed if that is the training unit; keep the same definitions.
